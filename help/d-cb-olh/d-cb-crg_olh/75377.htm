CacheFromDate

The cache maintains a CacheFromDate date for entities and entitlements.

For entities, CacheFromDate marks the start of the date range of entitlement mappings stored for the entity. This ensures that all:

  • Entitlement mappings with an effective end-date on or after this date, are stored in the cache.

    Entitlement mappings refer to the associations between entities (services or customer nodes) and entitlements.

  • Associated entitlements have a CacheFromDate on or before this date.

For entitlements, CacheFromDate marks the start of the date range of allocations currently stored for the entitlement. This ensures that all allocations associated with entitlements with an expiry date on or after this date, and with an available balance greater than zero, are stored in the cache.

CacheFromDate is updated when an entity is first accessed (the entity is not yet in the cache). When an entity is first accessed, it is retrieved from the database with all its related entitlements and their allocations that exist from the date-time of retrieval until the end-of-time. If a subsequent access is performed, and the access date-time precedes CacheFromDate, entitlements and allocations are retrieved for the missing period of time, that is, from the access date-time of this subsequent access until CacheFromDate. CacheFromDate is then updated to be equal to the earlier access date-time. If a request has an effective date outside of this range, it needs to be extended by retrieving additional information.

A CacheFromDate miss occurs when a request is made for a cached entity. The entity's CacheFromDate is not sufficient to satisfy the request's effective date, and data is inserted in the cache to extend the CacheFromDate.

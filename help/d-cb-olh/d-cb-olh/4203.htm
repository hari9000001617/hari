How Do I - Customer Care Tasks - Payments

Select the option most appropriate to the required task.

View a Payment

Search for and view details of a receipted payment using one of the following:

  • Receipt number
  • Account number into which the payment was made.
  • Name of the customer's account.

To search using a receipt number:

  1. Select the Accounts Payments Manage Individual Payments option. The Payment Detail Search form is displayed.
  2. Enter the receipt number in the Receipt Number field.
  3. Click the Search… button.

If the search returns:

  • A single record, the Individual Payment Detail form is displayed (if the Always Display List? option was not selected).
  • Multiple records, or the Always Display List? option is selected, the Payment Detail List form is displayed.

    To access the Individual Payment Detail form from this form, double-click the appropriate payment in the list of retrieved payments, or select the appropriate payment and click the Details… button. The Individual Payment Detail form is displayed detailing the specified payment.

To search using an account number into which the payment was made:

  1. Select the Accounts Accounts History option. The Account History form is displayed.
  2. Enter the customer's account number in the Account Number field.
  3. Click the Refresh History button. Details of all transactions associated with the specified account are listed.
  4. To view the details of a specific payment, double-click the appropriate payment, or highlight the payment and click the Detail... button. The Individual Payment Detail form is displayed detailing the specified payment.

To search for the customer account into which the payment was made:

  1. View the appropriate customer’s details.
  2. Click the Account History… button. The Account History form for the specified customer is displayed.
  3. To view the details of a specific payment, double-click the appropriate payment, or highlight the payment and click the Details... button. The Individual Payment Detail form is displayed detailing the specified payment.

Record a Payment

Payments can be entered:

  • Individually
  • In batches of the same payment type.

Before entering a payment, ensure that the following information is available:

  • Accurate information about the customer
  • Amount being paid
  • Payment type.

The details need to be recorded or verified to successfully enter a payment.

Tip:

When entering a payment, it is useful to have the Customer Detail form open for the customer whose account is being credited. Then the customer's account number can be copied from the Customer Detail form and pasted into the Account Number field on the relevant form.

Enter a Payment

To enter a payment:

  1. Select the Accounts Payment New Individual Payment option. The Individual Payment Detail form is displayed in Insert mode.
  2. Enter the payment details on the Details tab.

    The payment type selected determines the currency in which the payment is recorded and whether additional information needs to be entered in the fields on the General tab. The payment type's GL codes are displayed on the General tab.

    The Credit Account Number and Invoice Number fields are used for the account or invoice number to which the payment is being posted. Enter the account or invoice number and press the Tab key. The account or invoice number is verified against accounts held in the database and, if valid, the corresponding account name, account number, outstanding invoice number, and amount are displayed. If invalid, a warning message is displayed in the status bar. To rectify this, press the Delete key and enter a valid account or invoice number.

    If the payment is being made against a specific account (by entering the account number in the Credit Account Number field) the value that is automatically displayed in the Amount field does not include any unapplied payments or adjustments (for example, future-dated payments).

    If the payment is being made against a specific invoice (by entering the invoice number in the Invoice Number field) the value that is automatically displayed in the Amount field includes unapplied payments and adjustments.

    The date-time in the Date Received field defaults to the current date-time. The date is overridden by entering the required date either directly or using the drop-down calendar. The time is overridden by entering the required time; this may need to be done if the payment is entered retrospectively. If a future date is entered, the payment is not applied until the specified date.

    The Paid by group specifies the person who made the payment. If required, search for and assign a different contact as the payee (use the Search... and Assign buttons located in the Paid by group).

    For payments, the Invoice Number field reflects the current status. The word 'Credit' is displayed if there are no outstanding invoices, 'Split' is displayed if the payment was applied to a number of outstanding invoices or if the payment is allocated to one or more invoices and an amount is left over in credit.

  3. Select the General tab, and complete the appropriate data entry fields.
  4. Select the Invoices tab, and allocate the amount paid to one or more of the listed invoices.
  5. If appropriate, select the Debit Account Invoices tab and enter the appropriate amounts.
  6. If required, select the Up-Front Charges tab and enter the appropriate item and amount.
  7. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

    Clicking Save generates a receipt, assigns a receipt number to the payment, saves the payment details, and posts the payment amount to the specified account. Control is returned to the Individual Payment Detail form.

  8. If required, print the receipt.

Tip:

Rather than entering a number of payments individually, consider creating a batch for each type of payment and adding payments as they are received to the relevant payment batch. The payment batch can then be applied at the end of a predefined period (for example, at the end of the day, or at the end of a week). An organisation's business practices and billing cycles determine whether this is viable, and if so, the frequency at which payment batches need to be applied.

Allocate a Payment to an Invoice

When recording a payment, it (or part of the payment) is allocated to one or more outstanding invoices, or is simply recorded as a credit. Recording a payment as a credit (rather than against a specific invoice) may be necessary if, for example, there are no outstanding invoices or it is not obvious which invoice should be allocated the payment.

When a payment is made, Singleview automatically makes an initial allocation of these funds against any outstanding invoices (if there are any). The initial allocations can be modified before the payment is applied. Payments are allocated differently depending on the allocation function specified for the payment type.

After the initial allocation is made, view and update the allocations by selecting the Invoices tab (for an individual payment) or by selecting a payment and clicking the Invoices button (for a batch payment).

The amount allocated to each invoice and receivable type is displayed in the Amount field in the Individual Payment Detail form (for individual payments) or the This Payment field in the Invoices for Payment form (for batch payments). Any credit amount not allocated to an invoice is displayed in the Credit field. The Credit field is updated as funds are allocated or de-allocated to an invoice or receivable type.

To adjust the initial allocations made to the payment (whichever form is displayed):

  • Enter zero in the Amount or This Payment fields of the invoices that are not being allocated any funds, before allocating other invoices.
  • If the whole amount is being allocated against a single invoice, enter the full amount in the Amount or This Payment column of the appropriate invoice.
  • If the amount is being allocated against one or more receivable types, enter the amount in the Amount or This Payment column for the appropriate receivable type.
  • To split the payment between two or more invoices, enter the required amount for each invoice in the Amount or This Payment column. If the payment is more than the combined total of the outstanding invoice balances, the funds remaining are treated as a credit against the specified account.

Note:

If an amount that makes the total allocation amount greater than the total payment is being adjusted, do not leave the Amount or This Payment field. Enter zero in the Amount or This Payment fields of the invoices that are not being allocated any funds before allocating other invoices.

View and modify how a payment is allocated at any stage.

Modify a Payment Allocation

The allocation of a payment can be modified for:

  • An individual payment.
  • A payment within a payment batch.

To modify the allocation of an individual payment:

  1. View the appropriate payment.
  2. Select the Invoice tab. The allocations are displayed in the fields on the Invoice tab.
  3. Click the Update button on the toolbar.

  3. Click the Invoices button. The Invoices for Payment form is displayed for viewing the specified payment.
  4. Modify the allocation as required.
  5. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Generate a Receipt

A receipt for a customer payment can be generated for:

  • A payment recorded within a payment batch.
  • An individual payment.

Receipts (and associated receipt numbers) are generated automatically by Convergent Billing.

To generate a receipt for a payment recorded within a payment batch:

  1. Create a payment batch and record each payment (as required).
  2. Apply the payment batch. A receipt is generated by Convergent Billing for each payment in the batch.

Caution:

Although receipt numbers are allocated to payments as they are recorded, the payment batch must be applied for the receipts to be generated.

To generate a receipt for an individual payment, record and save the payment. A receipt is generated by Convergent Billing for the payment.

Reject a Payment

To reject a payment the appropriate security privileges must be available.

Why Reject a Payment?

Payments may need to be rejected. For example, a payment must be rejected if the customer's cheque is returned by the bank having been dishonoured because funds are not available in the customer's account, or the cheque is missing a signature. A credit card transaction also might not be approved because the payment would exceed the customer's credit limit, so the associated payment needs to be rejected.

To resolve these situations so that the transactions in the customer's account history reflect the account status, the payment needs to be rejected.

Note:

Rejecting a payment does not remove that transaction from a customer's account history. It inserts an additional transaction, a debit transaction equal to the amount of the original payment. Rejecting a payment allows reversal of the effect of posting a payment to a customer's account.

Tip:

A payment can only be rejected when it has been posted to a customer's account. Therefore, a payment made as part of a payment batch can only be rejected if that batch is applied. Payments included in a payment batch that is not yet applied can be removed from the batch.

To reject a payment:

  1. View the appropriate payment.
  2. Click the Reject Payment... button. The Payment Rejection Details form is displayed.

    Note:

    If rejecting a payment retrospectively, it may be necessary to change the rejection date.

  3. Select a rejection code appropriate to the reason for rejecting the payment.
  4. Unless specifically told to do so, do not change the default values in the Credit GL Code and Debit GL Code fields.
  5. Click the Save button to reject the payment. Control is returned to the Individual Payment Detail form, where the Payment Status field is now set to 'Rejected'.

Rejected payments are displayed as debits on the Account History form. The rejected payment is given the same receipt number as the original payment.

Delete a Payment

To delete a payment not yet posted to a customer's account:

  1. View the appropriate payment.
  2. Click the Delete button on the toolbar.
  3. When prompted, click the Yes button to confirm that the payment is to be deleted.

A payment posted to a customer's account can also be deleted using the same procedure if:

  • The payment is not included on (appeared on) an invoice or statement.
  • The payment has not been posted to the sales journal.
  • Security privileges to do so are available.

View a Payment Batch

Tip:

A search cannot be made for a payment included as part of a payment batch if the batch is not yet applied. To view details of such payments, view the payment batch, and then details of the required payment.

To search for a specific payment batch and list the payments in that batch:

  1. Select the Accounts Payments Manage Batch Payments option. The Payment Batch Search form is displayed.
  2. If known, enter the payment batch number in the Search Criteria field.
  3. Click the Search… button.

If the search returns:

  • A single record, the Payment Batch form is displayed (if the Always Display List? option is not selected).

    If a single payment batch record is returned from a search, the details of that record are displayed on the Payment Batch form. If more than one payment batch record is returned, the records are listed on the Payment Batch List form. The required record can then be selected and its details displayed on the Payment Batch form.

    If selected, the Always Display List? option overrides the automatic display of the Payment Batch form when only a single record is retrieved; in such cases the Payment Batch List form is displayed.

  • Multiple records, or the Always Display List? option is selected, the Payment Batch List form is displayed. To access the Payment Batch form from this form, double-click the appropriate batch in the list of retrieved batches, or select the appropriate batch and click the Details… button. The Payment Batch form is displayed listing each of the payments included in that batch.

Note:

If the payment batch is applied, view details of a specific payment by selecting that payment and clicking the Details... button (or by double-clicking the selected payment). The Individual Payment Detail form is displayed with the specified payment.

Create a Payment Batch

To create a payment batch:

  1. Collate payments into batches (groups) of the same payment type.
  2. Select the Accounts Payments New Batch Payment option. The Payment Batch form is displayed in Insert mode. Alternatively, the form can be displayed by selecting the Create a new batch option on the Payment Batch Search form and clicking the New... button.
  3. Enter a description of this batch in the Description field. The description entry is used to identify the batch at a later date.
  4. Select the appropriate payment type and batch type.
  5. Enter the account or invoice number associated with the payment and press the Tab key. A receipt number is assigned to the payment and displayed in the first field of the current row. The account or invoice number is verified against the account details and, if valid, corresponding account and invoice details are inserted into other fields. If invalid, a warning message is displayed on the Status bar. To rectify this, press the Delete button and enter a valid account or invoice number.

    Tip:

    Ensure that the customer name is correct. If not, a valid, but incorrect account or invoice number has probably been entered. To rectify this, press the Delete button to remove the row, click the Insert button to insert a new row, and re-enter the account or invoice number.

    Note:

    If the payment is being made against a specific account (by entering the account number in the Credit Account Number field), the value that is automatically displayed in the Amount field does not include any unapplied payments or adjustments (for example, future-dated payments). If the payment is being made against a specific invoice (by entering the invoice number in the Invoice field), the value that is automatically displayed in the Amount field includes unapplied payments or adjustments.

  6. If there is a single invoice outstanding against the account, the invoice number and the total amount appears in the Invoice and amount columns respectively. Enter the amount paid in the amount column and press the Tab key.
  7. If there are no outstanding invoices, the word 'Credit' is displayed in the Invoice column. Enter the amount paid in the amount column and press the Tab key.
  8. Click the Invoices… button. The Invoices for payment form is displayed in Insert mode.
  9. If there are a number of invoices against the account, the word 'Split' and the total amount outstanding appear in the Invoice and Amount columns respectively. The Individual Payment form is used to break up the payment between the outstanding invoices associated with the account.
  10. Allocate an amount to one or more of the listed invoices as required.
  11. Press the Tab key or close the Invoices for payment form.
  12. If necessary, change the date on which the payment was received. This may need to be done if payments are entered retrospectively.
  13. Select a value in the Payment Location column.
  14. Complete any other fields defined for this payment.

    Note:

    The fields displayed depend on the payment type selected for the batch. As each payment is entered, the total value of the payments in the batch is displayed on the lower right-hand corner of the form.

  15. Press the Down Arrow key to insert the next payment. A new row is inserted below the current row with a new receipt number.
  16. Repeat steps 5 to 12 until all payments have been entered, or until the batch needs to be saved.
  17. Click the Save button on the toolbar to save the details of the payment. A message confirming that the payment was inserted into the batch is displayed on the Status bar. The Payment Batch form is displayed in View Only mode.
  18. To post the payments in the payment batch to their respective accounts, apply the batch.
  19. To close the payment batch without posting the payments, click the Close button on the toolbar.

Un-apply a Payment Batch

Rules for Un-applying a Payment Batch

A payment batch can only be un-applied if a payment within that payment batch was not:

  • Included on (appeared on) an invoice or statement.
  • Posted to the Sales Journal.
  • Rejected.

To un-apply a payment batch:

  1. View the appropriate payment batch.
  2. Click the Un-apply button. The payments within the payment batch are un-applied and, if required, can be modified or deleted.

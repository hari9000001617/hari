Payments

Payments received from customers need to be recorded against their accounts. When recorded, those payments are reflected in that customer’s account details and subsequently on statements and invoices generated for that customer.

Legal document itemising charges (and possibly credits) accrued against that customer's account for services used by, or supplied to, that customer over a specified period. Accrued charges for a customer node in a customer hierarchy can be 'rolled up' into the invoice for a node higher in the customer hierarchy.

Context: Convergent Billing

See also Customer Hierarchy, Customer Node, Reporting Level, and Statement.

Document itemising charges (and possibly credits) accrued against a customer's account for services used by, or supplied to, that customer over a specified period. Unlike an invoice that itemises charges, a statement provides a record of the charges.

Context: Convergent Billing

See also Invoice and Reporting Level.

Unique account to which financial transactions are posted. Each customer has at least one account (known as the primary account) and optionally one or more secondary accounts. Each account has a unique account number by which it is identified.

See also Account Type.

Each payment is credited to the specified customer’s account. The payment can be made against an up-front charge or an outstanding invoice. If the payment is made against an invoice, that payment is allocated to the appropriate invoice. However, Convergent Billing’s flexibility allows a single payment to be split and allocated to multiple invoices. Multiple invoices are necessary when, for example, a customer pays four outstanding invoices with a single cheque. In fact, a payment can be allocated (fully or partially) to any outstanding invoice. In situations where no invoices are outstanding, the payment can be recorded as a credit.

Customers pay their invoices using a number of payment methods. For example, payments can be made by cash, cheque, credit card, or direct debit. Each of these payment methods can be recorded as payment types.

Payments can be entered individually, or in batches of the same payment type.

Convergent Billing assigns a receipt number to every recorded payment irrespective of whether that payment was entered as an individual payment or as part of a payment batch. Each payment batch is also assigned a payment batch number by which it can be referenced.

The payment type determines the format of each receipt number and payment batch number. Typically, the format for a receipt number includes a unique prefix so that, when viewing a customer’s account details, payments of a specific type can be more easily identified in the list of transactions.

If errors are discovered in a payment after it was posted (for example, the payment has been posted to the wrong account), facilities are provided to:

  • Change the allocation of the payment to an invoice.
  • Reject the payment.
  • Delete the payment.

Facilities are provided in Convergent Billing to search for and view specific payments and specific payment batches, to view the history of any changes to those payments, and if required, to preview and print the current receipt.

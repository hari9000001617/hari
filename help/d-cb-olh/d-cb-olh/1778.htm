Customer Hierarchies

A customer hierarchy is a representation of a customer’s hierarchical organisational or account structure. It consists of one or more customer nodes, each representing a customer account to which charges can be accrued. Corporate customers often require multiple billing/reporting points.

For example, if an account is required for each division in a multi-national company, a separate customer node is used to represent each division’s account.

The customer nodes in the customer hierarchy are organised into a structure (very often mirroring the company’s organisational structure) that is similar to a family tree. Like a family tree, a customer hierarchy displays the relationship between customer nodes.

A relationship exists between Node A and the two nodes directly below it, namely Nodes B and C. In terms of the hierarchy, Node A is said to be the parent of Nodes B and C. Nodes B and C are said to be children of Node A. A similar parent-child relationship exists between Node B and Nodes D and E, and between Node C and Nodes F and G. Notice that Node B is both a child (of Node A) and a parent (of Nodes D and E). Similarly, Node C is both a child (of Node A) and a parent (of Nodes F and G).

The node at the top of the customer hierarchy is unique in that it has no parent node. It is considered as the root of the hierarchy and is referred to as the root customer node.

Associated with each customer node within the hierarchy is a reporting level, which determines whether that node is to receive an invoice, statement, or neither. The root customer node must have a reporting level of ‘Invoice’. A customer can receive either a statement or an invoice if the required invoice and statement formats are defined.

Convergent Billing provides a facility to display single or multiple instances of a customer hierarchy using the Customer Search form. Convergent Billing provides facilities to create, view, and modify each customer node forming a specific customer’s accounting hierarchy. In addition, facilities are provided to re-organise the hierarchy to meet changing customer requirements.

For more information about configuring the Customer Hierarchy form, refer to the Customer Care and Product Configuration Guide for Singleview Convergent Billing.

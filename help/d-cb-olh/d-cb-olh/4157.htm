Create a Product Instance

Products are typically implemented with product offers, to simplify product configuration and product instance creation.

If a Product Offer drop-down list is not displayed on the Product Instance Detail form, the product is not associated with a product offer. In this case, follow procedure 2 to create the product instance.

Procedure 1

To create a product instance from a product associated with a product offer:

  1. Assign a product to a customer or customer node.
  2. Select a product offer to use with the product instance. The Offer tab is displayed.
  3. Expand the Offer Hierarchy tree, if required.
  4. Select optional child product offers and companion products by ticking the check boxes beside their entry in the tree. Mandatory child product offers and companion products will be selected automatically.

    Note:

    Placement of a companion product in the tree may affect the charges and entitlements configured for the product. Check configuration in the Companion Products tab to ensure that the desired settings are available.

  5. Click the Apply Changes button. The Companion Products tab is displayed.
  6. Select each of the companion products displayed, and add any further configuration information required.
  7. Allocate equipment to each service (where required).
  8. Create the required services.

    Note:

    One service must be created before the product instance is complete and can become active.

  9. If required, select one or more of the available optional product components.
  10. If required, select one or more of the available lists on the Customer Detail or Service Detail forms and create list items for each of those lists.
  11. To save the product instance, click the Save button on the toolbar.

Procedure 2

To create a product instance from a product not associated with a product offer:

  1. Assign a product to a customer or customer node.
  2. Allocate required equipment to each service.
  3. Create the required services.

    Note:

    One service must be created before the product instance is complete and can become active.

  4. If required, select one or more of the available optional product components.
  5. If required, select one or more of the available facility groups.
  6. If required, select one or more of the available companion products.
  7. If required, select one or more of the available lists on the Customer Detail or Service Detail forms and create list items for each of those lists.
  8. If required, record any up-front charges associated with the product.
  9. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Assign a Product to a Customer

To assign a product to a customer:

  1. Ensure that the name of the customer to be assigned a product is displayed in the list of customers in the left-hand panel of the Customer Hierarchy form.
  2. Select the product group on which the icon associated with the product to be assigned is displayed.
  3. Drag and drop the appropriate base product icon from the selected product group onto the customer's name. Companion products cannot be dragged and dropped.

    Icon associated with a specific base product. The same icon can be used for more than one product. Product icons are displayed in product groups on the Customer Hierarchy form.

    Context: Convergent Billing

    See also Base Product and Product Group.

    When dropped onto the appropriate customer name, the Product Instance Detail form is displayed in Insert mode.

  4. Create a product instance of the base product.
  5. Save and close the Product Instance Detail form. Summary details for the product and its associated services are listed in the right-hand panel of the Customer Hierarchy form.

    Tip:

    If an attempt is made to close the form without creating each of the associated services, or select the required product offer, child product offers, and companion products, Convergent Billing displays a message warning that the product is not yet complete. Either return to the form and create the remaining services, or ignore the message and complete the product at a later date.

    Note:

    A product cannot be made active until it is completed.

Delete a Product Instance

Caution:

A product instance can only be removed/deleted if it has a status of Product Creation. If a product instance is not in this state, deleting a product instance is identical to cancelling that instance.

To delete an incomplete product instance (a product instance with a status of Product Creation):

  1. View the appropriate product instance.
  2. Click the Delete button on the toolbar.

  4. When prompted, click the Yes button to confirm that the product instance is to be deleted. The product instance and all associated services are deleted.

Change Product Offer Associated with a Product Instance

When the product offer associated with a base product instance is changed, field defaults configured in the new product offer overwrite the previous product offer's field defaults. This may affect behaviour of the base product or companion product instances associated with the product offer.

To change the product offer associated with a product instance:

  1. View the appropriate product instance.
  2. Select the new product offer from the Product Offer drop-down list. The Offer tab is displayed.
  3. Expand the Offer Hierarchy tree, if required.
  4. Select optional child product offers and companion products by ticking the check boxes beside their entry in the tree. Mandatory child product offers and companion products will be selected automatically.

    Note:

    Placement of a companion product in the tree may affect the charges and entitlements configured for the product. Check configuration in the Companion Products tab to ensure that the desired settings are available.

  5. Click the Apply Changes button. The Companion Products tab is displayed.
  6. Select each of the companion products displayed, and add any further configuration information required.

    Note:

    Instances of companion products available in the previous product offer hierarchy are merged to the new product offer hierarchy. Instances of companion products no longer required in the new product offer hierarchy are cancelled.

  7. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Upgrade a Product Instance

Note:

Options on the Upgrade/Move Details tab of the Product Instance Detail form cannot be selected while creating a product instance. Product instances that are created as part of a product offer, or have been previously moved, upgraded, or cancelled, cannot be upgraded.

To upgrade a product:

  1. View the appropriate product instance.
  2. Select the Upgrade/Move Details tab.
  3. Click the Update button on the toolbar. The Product Instance Detail form is displayed in Update mode.
    • The From Product button is enabled if the product was merged or is the result of an upgrade.
    • The To Product button is only enabled if the product instance was previously upgraded.
    • Clicking the From Product button displays details of the product from which the product instance being viewed was upgraded or moved.
    • Clicking the To Product button displays details of the product to which the product instance being viewed was upgraded or moved.
  4. Click the Upgrade button. A message is displayed prompting selection of the product to which the current product instance is to be upgraded.
  5. Select the Detail tab and, if necessary, update the services required to make the new product instance active.
  6. Complete the appropriate fields on the Facility Groups and Companion Product tabs for the upgraded product instance.
  7. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Split a Product Instance Manually

Notes and Restrictions

The following terminology is used in the procedures given for splitting and merging product instances:
  • Source product instance (from which services or equipment are to be allocated)
  • Target product instance (to which services and equipment in the source product instance are allocated).

A product instance can only be split or merged if:

  • It was not created as part of a product offer.
  • Update security privileges for the Product Instance Detail form are available.
  • Compatible base products are available.
  • The product is configured to allow splitting or merging.

To split a product instance manually:

  1. View the appropriate (source) product instance.
  2. Select the Upgrade/Move Details tab and click the Update button. The Upgrade button on the tab is enabled.
  3. Click the Upgrade button. A message is displayed prompting selection of the target product.
  4. Select the target product from the drop-down list and click the Yes button. The Product Upgrade: Allocation form is displayed.
  5. To allocate all source product instance components, click the Allocate All button. All components in the source product instance are allocated to the specified target product instance. The Upgrade Status field for each of these components is now set to Used.
  6. To allocate individual source product components, select one of the source product instance components and the equivalent target product instance component and click the Allocate button. The Upgrade Status field for this component is now set to Used.
  7. Repeat this procedure for each component required by the target product instance. If a component is incorrectly allocated, select it and click the Deallocate button.
  8. Click the Done button. A message is displayed to:
    • Indicate the number of source product instance components allocated and the number of components still to be allocated.
    • Prompt whether the remaining components should be allocated to another product instance.
  9. Click the Yes button. A message is displayed prompting selection of the next target product instance.
  10. Repeat steps 4 to 7 for the next target product instance.

When all components of the source product instance have been allocated, the source product instance is split and each of the target product instances are saved with their new components.

On completion of the split, the source product instance is cancelled when each of the target product instances becomes active.

Split a Product Instance Automatically

Notes and Restrictions

The following terminology is used in the procedures given for splitting and merging product instances:
  • Source product instance (from which services or equipment are to be allocated)
  • Target product instance (to which services and equipment in the source product instance are allocated).

A product instance can only be split or merged if:

  • It was not created as part of a product offer.
  • Update security privileges for the Product Instance Detail form are available.
  • Compatible base products are available.
  • The product is configured to allow splitting or merging.

To split a product instance automatically:

  1. View the appropriate (source) product instance.
  2. Select the Upgrade/Move Details tab and click the Update button. The Upgrade button on the tab is now enabled.
  3. Click the Upgrade button. A message is displayed prompting selection of the target product.
  4. Select the target product from the drop-down list and click the Yes button. A further message is displayed to:
    • Indicate the number of source product instance components allocated and components still to be allocated.
    • Specify whether to allocate the remaining components to another product instance.
  5. Click the Yes button. A message is displayed prompting selection of the next target product instance.
  6. Select the target product instance from the drop-down list and click the Yes button.
  7. Repeat steps 4 to 6 until the product split is complete. On completion, the Product Instance Detail form is displayed in View Only mode for the last target product instance.

On completion of the split, the source product instance is cancelled when each of the target product instances becomes active.

Merge a Product Instance

Notes and Restrictions

The following terminology is used in the procedures given for splitting and merging product instances:
  • Source product instance (from which services or equipment are to be allocated)
  • Target product instance (to which services and equipment in the source product instance are allocated).

A product instance can only be split or merged if:

  • It was not created as part of a product offer.
  • Update security privileges for the Product Instance Detail form are available.
  • Compatible base products are available.
  • The product is configured to allow splitting or merging.

To merge a product instance:

  1. Assign the target product to the appropriate customer by dragging the product icon onto the customer listed in the Customer Hierarchy form. The Product Instance Detail form is displayed.
  2. On the Detail tab, select one of the service component entries and click the Allocate button. A message is displayed prompting for confirmation that the product dates are correct.
  3. Click the Yes button. A list of services associated with product instances of the specified product type assigned to this customer is displayed on the Service List form.
  4. Select the service to be merged into the target product instance and click the Assign button. The Product Upgrade: Allocation form is displayed. The selected service component is allocated to the target product instance. Either allocate all the source product instance components to the target product instance or allocate individual source product instance components.
  5. To allocate all source product instance components, click the Allocate All button. All components in the source product instance are allocated to the specified target product instance. The Upgrade Status field for each of these components is now set to 'Used'.
  6. To allocate individual source product components, select one of the source product instance components and the equivalent target product instance component and click the Allocate button. The Upgrade Status field for this component is now set to 'Used'. Repeat this procedure for each component required by the target product instance. If a component is incorrectly allocated, select it and click the Deallocate button.
  7. Click the Done button. The Product Instance Detail form is displayed for the target product instance.

    To perform a full merge, repeat steps 2 to 6 for each of the other services required by the target product instance, then save the target product instance. The Status field of the target product instance is now set to 'Active'; the source product instances are set to 'Cancelled'.

    To perform a partial merge, create each of the other services required by the target product instance, allocate equipment (where necessary), then save the target product instance.

Move a Product Instance between Customer Nodes

If this is the first move since logging in, the Move Preferences form is displayed automatically. If not, and move preferences need to be changed, they can be changed manually. The following procedure assumes that move preferences have been set.

When moving product instances between nodes in different customer hierarchies, use multiple instances of the Customer Hierarchy form to display both customer hierarchies simultaneously.

Restrictions on Moving a Product Instance

The following restrictions apply when moving a product instance:
  • A product cannot be upgraded or moved if the product instance was created as part of a product offer.
  • A product cannot be moved if its owning customer or target customer is blacklisted at any time during the move period and the user does not have access to view blacklisted customers on the Customer Form.
  • A product cannot be upgraded or moved if the product instance is cancelled, moved, or upgraded at the effective start date or any later date.
  • A product must be assigned to a customer node. A product dragged over a root node or blank space of the hierarchy panel cannot be dropped.
  • Some information regarding the lists and charge categories may need to be entered (when prompted) before the product instance move can be completed.

Caution:

If a product instance is moved, any changes made to it before or after the move start date are not carried over in the move. For example, if the product instance is moved at an effective date before an equipment allocation change, following the move the variation of equipment allocation is not reflected. A warning is displayed so the user can cancel the move.

To move a product instance:

  1. Display the source node in the left-hand panel of the first instance of the Customer Hierarchy form. Ensure that the details for the source node are displayed in the right-hand panel. Select the Products option from the Display for Customer group to ensure that the source product is displayed.
  2. Display the target node in the left-hand panel of the second instance of the Customer Hierarchy form.
  3. Drag and drop the source product instance (located in the right-hand panel of the first instance of the Customer Hierarchy form) onto the target node (displayed in the left-hand panel of the second instance of the Customer Hierarchy form). Ensure that the product instance and not the associated service is dragged and dropped.

    To drag and drop a product instance, click the product instance and, while holding down the left mouse button, move the cursor into the left-hand panel until the target node is highlighted. Now drop the product instance onto the target node by releasing the mouse button.

    As the product instance is dragged from the right-hand to the left-hand panel, the cursor changes from one depicting a stop symbol to one depicting an arrow. If the icon is dropped when the stop symbol is displayed, no action is taken.

  4. The Move Confirm form is displayed. Enter the appropriate date range and click the Continue button.

Set Product Instance Move Preferences

Depending on type of move, preferences can be set to determine how the move is performed.

If multiple product instances are being moved, and one or more of the instances fail to move, preferences can be set to do the following:

  • Cancel all moves.
  • Move the instances that can be moved and display a report detailing which moves failed and why.

The Move Preferences form is displayed automatically the first time a move is performed after logging in. If the preferences need to be set again, the form can be accessed manually.

To set move preferences manually:

  1. Display the Customer Hierarchy form.
  2. Select the Customer Hierarchy Move Preferences option. The Move Preferences form is displayed.
  3. To cancel all moves if one fails, select 'Back-out all moves' in the On failed move group.
  4. To move all instances possible and to display a move report once the move is complete, select 'Continue and show Move Report' after all items have been processed' from the On failed move group.
  5. Click OK.

  10. Complete steps 2 to 6 for each additional component to be allocated to the product instance.
  11. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Cancel a Service

Caution:

  • Cancelling a service cancels the whole product instance.
  • Restrictions apply when cancelling a service.

To cancel a specific service:

  1. View the service using one of the available methods (summary or detailed).
  2. Click the Delete button on the toolbar. A Confirm window is displayed.
  3. Change the date, if required, and click the Yes button.

To cancel all services associated with a specific product instance, cancel the product instance.

The following applies when cancelling services:

  • Delete button is enabled only if the service can be cancelled within the service's record date range.
  • Clicking the Delete button for services that do not have a status of Product Creation, cancels the service.
  • Minimum cancel date is set to the earliest date at which the service becomes optional, and remains optional until the end of time. The earliest possible cancel date is the service record's effective start date.
  • Default cancel date is set as follows:
    • If the maximum number of permitted services for the service's item exceeds the number allowed by the product instance or the companion instance settings and remains at that level until the end of time, the earliest date at which this occurs is used.
    • If the service becomes optional at some future date and remains optional until the end of time, that date is used.
    • If the service is optional as of today's date and remains optional until the end of time, today's date is used.
  • If a service becomes optional, then mandatory, then optional again, only the last optional period can be cancelled.

Delete a Service

Note:

Restrictions apply when deleting a service.

To delete a service:

  1. View the service using one of the available methods (summary or detailed).
  2. Click the Delete button on the toolbar. A Confirm window is displayed.
  3. Change the date, if required, and click the Yes button. The service status changes to Cancelled.

Note:

Using the Delete button is the preferred method for deletions. The following applies:

  • Clicking the Delete button for services that have a status of Product Creation deletes the service.
  • The date from which to delete the service must be equal to the effective start date of the service.

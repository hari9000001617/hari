Contacts

Details can be recorded about any person, position, or organisation that, during normal business, may need to be contacted.

One of the main reasons for recording a contact is to assign that contact to a customer, which can be regarded as simply creating a 'link' between that contact and a specified customer. The link effectively defines a relationship between contact and customer. If the link is no longer required, the link can be removed by unassigning the contact.

The same contact can be assigned to more than one customer. Each assignment is independent of each of the other assignments. Unassigning a contact therefore has no effect on the links to any other customer. Unassigning a contact simply removes the link between the contact and a specified customer and is therefore not the same as deleting the contact

A contact can be assigned as a primary customer contact (typically the person to whom the customer’s invoice is sent) or as a secondary customer contact.

To ensure integrity of customer data, the system only permits a primary customer contact to be replaced or modified. A contact assigned as a primary customer contact can therefore only be deleted after it is replaced (if it is not specified as a primary customer contact for another customer). A contact assigned as a secondary customer contact can be unassigned, replaced, modified, or deleted whenever required.

Details for a contact can be maintained whenever required. For example, update a contact by modifying or deleting details, or adding details. If a contact is no longer required, it can also be deleted (if it is not assigned as a contact for a customer).

Contact Examples

This facility is usually used to record details of a person to be assigned as the contact for a customer; for example, recording details of the Accounts Payable clerk of a corporate customer who is responsible for handling invoices.

In many organisations, a function such as Accounts Payable is carried out by a number of different people. In such cases, it is more appropriate to record the details of a specific position, rather than a specific person.

Details of any organisation to be contacted during normal day-to-day activities can be recorded. It could be another department or division within a company, or an external organisation. For example, as a result of a fault inquiry, an organisation that services a specific equipment item has to be contacted to arrange a customer callout, or the address and phone number of a specified equipment supplier’s Help Desk may need to be recorded.

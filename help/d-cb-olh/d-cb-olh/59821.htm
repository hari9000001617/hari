Facility Groups

Note:

Facility groups are not compatible with product offers.

When a service instance is created as part of an overall product package, one or more service options can be included. For example, if a GSM mobile phone service is included as part of the product package to a business customer, customer might also be offered Call Waiting and Call Diversion as service options.

In Convergent Billing, a service option is referred to as a facility. Facilities can be offered individually or in combination, but must be offered as part of a service option package, more commonly referred to in Convergent Billing as a facility group. A facility group must contain at least one facility.

Facilities cannot be added to a product directly; they can only be defined in a facility group and are commonly regarded as attributes of the service instance to which they apply. A facility is defined and stored in the Convergent Billing database as a reference type of type 'FACILITY'.

In addition to facilities, a facility group can include one or more compatible facility groups that can be used in conjunction with (configured with) the current facility group.

In addition to determining the number, content, and compatibility of the facility groups to be provided, whether those facility groups are to be selected automatically by Convergent Billing when the service is created or selected manually must also be determined.

If automatic facility group selection is specified, the user has no control over the facility groups associated with the service they are creating. If manual facility group selection is specified, the user can decide on whether to select a specific facility group for the service they are creating. This decision is usually based on what the customer has requested.

Tips for Designing Facility Groups

When designing a facility group, careful consideration is required about which facilities to include, because incorrectly packaging facilities may prove to be too inflexible for customer requirements.

For example, a facility group that includes both Call Waiting and Call Diversion as facilities is defined. When this option is offered to a customer, that customer only has the choice of accepting the whole facility group (that is, they accept both facilities) or rejecting the facility group (in which case they get neither facility).

The solution in this case is to create two facility groups, each containing a single facility.

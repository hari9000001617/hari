Billing

During this stage, the normalised events and charges output by the rating engine are processed by the billing engine to generate billing data records.

Essentially, the billing engine is identical to the rating engine in that it applies tariffs to the input data to generate the output data and supports parallel tariffing, but there are two major differences.

Firstly, unlike the rating engine (which applies tariffs to individual data records), the billing engine applies tariffs to aggregated data records. Aggregation means that the individual charges are aggregated (grouped) into one or more predefined groups for the purpose of obtaining a total value or charge for that grouping. In general, charges are aggregated according to the predefined customer hierarchy so that totals can be obtained for normalised events, services, individual and child customer nodes, and customers.

Secondly, the tariffs applied to the aggregated records are generally discount tariffs (benefits) rather than charge tariffs. For example, if a customer's usage is higher than a pre-set limit for the specified billing cycle then a volume discount could be applied to the total cost of the calls.

In addition, it is possible to generate:

  • Invoices, which are sent out to the customer for payment.
  • Temporary statements, which can be used for quality assurance purposes or for customer information.

Financial Reporting

During billing, the BGP creates GL guidance records to:

  • Map the correct GL codes to invoice transactions for rental and rental adjustment events.
  • Record the state changes for rating charges. The BGP tracks the financial state change of a charge when an invoice is applied to a customer account.

Invoice receivable type history records are created based on the amounts aggregated to each accounts receivable GL code for Billed and Earned (BE) charges for each invoice.

For more information about GL guidance records and invoice receivable types, refer to the Finance Guide for Singleview.

Sales and Cash Receipts Journal

A Sales and Cash Receipts Journal (in Singleview, referred to as a Sales Journal) tracks financial transactions related to sales to customers, including payments, adjustments, and credit. Transactions are associated with GL codes (for sales, indirectly using a GL guidance entity).

GL codes are usually defined in a hierarchical manner to group similar transactions for summarisation at varying levels. In the Convergent Billing database, GL codes are associated with a reporting entity, providing a separate sales journal for each reporting entity.

Convergent Billing internally groups and records sales journal transactions, and associates the transactions with an accounting period. At the end of each accounting period, sales journal entries can be extracted and posted to an external general ledger system.

The Sales Journal records the following types of transactions:

  • Revenue for all sales made against a Convergent Billing account, which are rated or billed by Convergent Billing.
  • Payments made to customer accounts, including payments against postpaid and prepaid accounts.
  • Adjustments made against customer accounts.

The Sales Journal also reflects the change of state of a transaction; for example, the change of state from deferred (Billed but Unearned (BUE)) to recognised (Billed and Earned (BE)) revenue.

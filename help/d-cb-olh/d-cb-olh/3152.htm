Services

A service is a specific component of a product for which the customer is charged. Each service has an associated service type, and unique service number against which usage is charged. One or more facilities (service options) and/or items of equipment may be associated with each service.

Physical components required to provide a service; for example, modem, leased line, or mobile phone. Equipment information is stored for information purposes only, but can also be used to allocate service numbers.

Context: Convergent Billing, Customer Management

See also Service.

Service option (for example, Call Waiting). A facility can be regarded as a logical attribute of a service. Facilities cannot be included in a product directly; they must be defined as part of a facility group. Usage of a facility is billed against the corresponding service.

Context: Convergent Billing, Customer Management, Configuration Studio

See also Facility Group, Facility Group Compatibility, and Service.

Specifies any defaults or entity validation for services specified with that type. If required, an icon can be specified to identify each service type.

Context: Convergent Billing, Configuration Studio

See also Icon and Service.

Refer to Base Product and Companion Product.

A service can be created while assigning a product instance to a customer, or at some later time. Until each of the services associated with a product is created, that product cannot be made active.

Specific combination of services and the associated tariff structure offered to a customer based on the framework provided by the corresponding product definition. The original product definition is preserved, with the product instance being tailored to a specific customer's requirements. Any changes made to the product definition affect only product instances created after the changes are made, not existing product instances.

Referred to as a Purchased Product in Customer Management.

Context: Convergent Billing, Customer Management

See also Product Definition and Product Model.

Services can be added to product instances cancelled at a future date. However, services cannot be added to product instances that have a future status of Moved or Upgraded.

A service cannot be updated to a status of Product Creation, and cannot be updated when it has a status of Product Creation or Cancelled.

A service cannot be cancelled if it leaves the product instance incomplete as of the cancellation date or in the future. Services are automatically cancelled if the product instance is cancelled.

A service can only be cancelled if it:

  • Is in View Only mode.
  • Has no future service records.
  • Is not in a status of Product Creation, Cancelled, Moved, or Upgraded.
  • Has no unallocated service or equipment items associated with the base product instance (that is, the base product instance is Active).

A service can be deleted only if it:

  • Does not have future service records.
  • Is in a status of Product Creation.
  • Is in View Only mode.

How Do I - Customer Care Tasks - Customer Accounts

Select the option most appropriate to the required task.

View an Account

The method to access and view a customer’s account depends on whether the account number is known.

If the account number is known:

  1. Select the Accounts Account History option. The Account History form is displayed.
  2. Enter the account number in the Account Number field.
  3. Press Tab or click the Refresh History button. Transaction details associated with the specified account are displayed including the account balance.

If the account number is not known:

  1. View the Customer Details Form for the customer.
  2. To view the primary account, click the Account History… button. The Account History form is displayed with details of the primary account including the account balance.
  3. To view a secondary account, click the Other Accounts… button. The Other Accounts for Customer form is displayed listing the secondary accounts (if any) assigned to the specified customer.
  4. Double-click the account to be viewed or select the account and click the Account History… button. The Account History form is displayed with details of the specified secondary account.

Create a Primary Account

A primary account is created when the details for a new customer are recorded in the system. The primary account number can either be entered manually (when other details are recorded) or assigned to the customer automatically by Singleview Convergent Billing. The format of an automatically assigned primary account number is determined by the account type.

Create a Secondary Account

To create a secondary account:

  1. View the Customer Details form for the appropriate customer.
  2. Click the Update button on the toolbar.
  3. Click the Other Accounts... button. The Other Accounts for Customer form is displayed listing the secondary accounts (if any) assigned to the specified customer.
  4. Click the Insert button on the toolbar. The Other Accounts for Customer form is displayed in Update mode.

    Note:

    Depending on privileges and the start date of the record, a Confirm window may be displayed.

    Update privileges required for types of update:

    Type of Update

    Privilege Required

    Used To

    Normal

    Update

    Update current and future records.

    Historical Update

    Update an historical record, or the current record and backdate the changes.

    Date range

    Date Range Update

    Modify the date range of an historical record.

     

  5. Enter the account number (if known) and/or select the account type. Multiple accounts of the same type can be added to a customer node, if allowed by the account type.
  6. Click the Save button on the toolbar. The account details are saved and the form is reopened in View Only mode. If an account number is not entered, Convergent Billing assigns a number before saving the account details.
  7. To enter additional secondary accounts, repeat Steps 3 to 5.
  8. Close the Other Accounts form.

View an Account Transaction

To view an account transaction:

  1. View the account history for the relevant customer.
  2. Display the required transaction entry in the lower part of the form by scrolling through the list of transactions.
  3. Double-click the transaction to be viewed, or select the transaction and click the Details… button. The appropriate form is displayed in View Only mode with details of the selected transaction.

Record a Payment

Payments can be entered:

  • Individually
  • In batches of the same payment type.

Before entering a payment, ensure that the following information is available:

  • Accurate information about the customer
  • Amount being paid
  • Payment type.

The details need to be recorded or verified to successfully enter a payment.

Tip:

When entering a payment, it is useful to have the Customer Detail form open for the customer whose account is being credited. Then the customer's account number can be copied from the Customer Detail form and pasted into the Account Number field on the relevant form.

Need more information about payments?

Record an Adjustment

Adjustments can be entered in either of the following ways:

  • Individually
  • In batches of the same adjustment type.

Before entering an adjustment, ensure that accurate information about the customer is available, amount of the adjustment, and the adjustment type. The details need to be recorded or verified to successfully enter an adjustment.

Tip:

When entering an adjustment, it is useful to have the Customer Detail form open for the customer getting the adjustment. The customer's account number can then be copied from the Customer Detail form and pasted into the Account Number field on the relevant form.

Delete an Account

Rules for Deleting a Secondary Account.

To delete a secondary account:

  • Security privileges must be available to delete the record.
  • The account balance must be zero.
  • No future records for this customer that specify this account must exist.

Caution:

Primary accounts cannot be deleted.

To delete a secondary account:

  1. View the appropriate account history.
  2. Click the Delete button on the toolbar.
  3. When prompted, click the Yes button to confirm that the account is to be deleted.

View an Account Type

To view an account type:

  1. Select the Maintenance Accounts Account Types option. The Account Type Search form is displayed.
  2. Enter or select the appropriate search criteria and search method.
  3. Click the Search button. A list of account types that match the specified criteria is displayed.
  4. Select the appropriate account type and click the Detail... button, or double-click the appropriate account type. The Account Type Definition form is displayed in View Only mode.

Need more information on account types?

Define an Account Type

To define an account type:

  1. Select the Maintenance Accounts Account Types option. The Account Type Search form is displayed.
  2. Click the New... button. The Account Type Definition form is displayed in Insert mode.
  3. Complete the appropriate data entry fields on the form.
  4. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Need more information on account types?

Delete a Reservation

To delete a non-volatile reservation:

  1. View the account history for an account associated with the reservation.
  2. Select the Reservations tab and highlight the reservation to be deleted.
  3. Click the Delete button on the toolbar.
  4. When prompted, click the Yes button to confirm that the reservation is to be deleted.

Contracts

Special terms and conditions can be applied to a set of products and services for any root customer by using contracts. Any services associated with a root customer that are related to the products used for the contract, can be linked to the contract (except if they are used by another contract). Convergent Billing also offers the option of associating all the customer's available services with the contract.

Legal agreement between the organisation and the customer to apply special terms and conditions to products and services for a specific duration.

Context: Convergent Billing

Provides a specific component of the overall product for which the customer is charged. Each service has an associated service type, and unique service number against which usage is charged. One or more facilities (service options) or items of equipment, or both, can be associated with each service.

Context: Convergent Billing

See also Equipment, Facility, and Service Type.

Refer to Base Product and Companion Product.

A contract has one or more base products and product offers associated with it. Each companion product in a product offeris used to modify the costing attributes of the base product for the term of the contract.

The contract functionality enhances the ability to offer:

  • Products at a discount price for a specific period of time.
  • Discounts for selected ranges of numbers (for example, a company's calls between offices).
  • Fixed prices for specific products.
  • Specific contracts to meet requirements for an individual customer.

For example, a twelve month contract is defined to offer a discount on mobile calls to large corporate customers if a certain usage for their standard business touch phones is reached. A companion product is defined with a discount tariff that overrides the usage tariffs (calculates a negative amount to counteract the usage tariff), and any subtotals, specified in the base product. Tariff eligibility criteria is applied to determine whether the set amount of usage is reached and whether or not the discount is applied. A product offer is created containing the discount companion product, and set as a child of the product offer associated with the base product.

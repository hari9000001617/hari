Balance Management

Balance management is the process by which real-time account management is achieved.

The following balance management services are possible:

  • Real-time service authorisation

    Event authorisation provides real-time account balance management. When an event is established, and during pre-determined intervals within an event, the balance manager uses cached service, customer, account and entitlement information to determine the appropriate credit amount to reserve for that event. Once the event is complete, the unused reserved amount is returned to the customer's account credit, so that the next event can be accurately processed.

  • Customer controlled account selection and advice of charge

    Subscribing customers can control which of their accounts is used on a per session basis. A subscriber can request the cost of a specific item or service, including rates for volume-based or duration-based services.

  • Customer controlled service-specific spending limits

    Subscribing customers can specify spending limits specific to a service. For example, a mobile phone can be limited to a specified amount per month or restricted from making international calls.

  • Real-time rating

    Both event establishment and event completion are immediately communicated and processed. Account credits and credit limits are updated as events occur.

  • Real-time credit limit checks

    Credit limits are checked when events are generated for real-time tariffs, reservations are created against account balances, and the unbilled amount of the account is updated with a charge generated by a tariff configured for real-time account aggregation.

  • Consistent rating performance

    The rating engine performs consistently to provide real time account processing and queries. Caches can be pre-loaded. Cache misses are prevented during dynamic configuration or system re-start.

  • High and continuous availability of Commerce Engine

    A system that offers cost authorisation and advice of charge information needs to be effectively and consistently available, and should not be disturbed by planned or unplanned outages. Redundant components and multiple processes are supported for both Commerce Engine and user transactions. Both Convergent Billing and third party upgrades can be performed without interruption of the Commerce Engine service.

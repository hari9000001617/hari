How Do I - Customer Care Tasks - Contacts

Select the option most appropriate to the required task.

View a Contact's Details

Contact details can be searched for and viewed using different methods:

  • Search for, and view details of, a specific contact.
  • Determine, and view details of, the contacts assigned to a specific customer.

The chosen method depends on what the primary requirement is for obtaining the information.

Need more information on contacts?

Search for a Contact's Details

To search for a contact:

  1. Select the Customer Care People Manage People option. The Person Search form is displayed.
  2. Enter the appropriate search criteria and click the Search… button. If explicit criteria are entered, fewer contact records matching those criteria are returned.

    Tip:

    The Family Name and Given Name fields can be used interchangeably. Therefore, search for a family name by entering it in the Family Name field or Given Name field.

    If the search returns:

    • A single contact, the Person Detail form is displayed in View Only mode (if the Always Display List? option was not selected).

      If a single contact record is returned from a search, the details of that contact are displayed on the Person Detail form. If more than one contact record is returned, the queries are listed on the Person List form. The required contact record can then be selected and its details displayed on the Person Detail form.

      If selected, the Always Display List? option overrides the automatic display of the Person Detail form when only a single record is retrieved; in such cases the Person List form is displayed.

    • Multiple contacts or the Always Display List? option was selected, the Person List form is displayed.
  3. To access the Person Detail form from the Person List form, double-click the appropriate contact record in the list of retrieved contacts, or select the appropriate record and click the Details… button.

Note:

If, for example, the person has a status of Bad Risk, the entry for that person in the Person List form is displayed as greyed-out. That display indicates that the person may be unsuitable as the primary contact for a customer; it does not indicate that there are restrictions imposed on the use of that person's details. The company's business rules and practices determine whether a person having a status of Bad Risk can be assigned to a customer or be assigned queries.

View Contacts for a Customer

Determine the contacts assigned to a customer using either of the following forms:

  • Customer Hierarchy
  • Customer Detail

The contacts assigned to a specific customer are listed in the right-hand panel of the Customer Hierarchy form, if the Contacts display option is selected. Details for each of the assigned contacts can then be viewed.

Display options are used to select the items displayed in the right-hand panel for the customer or customer node selected in the left-hand panel.

Note:

If more than one contact is assigned to the customer, one of the contacts has the status of Primary; other contacts may display a status other than Primary, depending on the status set (if any) for that contact on the Customer Contacts form.

To search for a contact using the Customer Hierarchy form:

  1. Select the Customer Care Customers Manage Customers option. The Customer Search form is displayed.
  2. Enter appropriate search criteria to retrieve the required customer, ensure that the Always Display Hierarchy? option is selected, and click the Search… button. The Customer Hierarchy form is displayed listing the customers returned by the search.
  3. Click the customer name in the left-hand panel. The items (contacts, products, services, equipment, queries, and contracts) associated with the customer are listed in the right-hand panel (if all available display options are selected).
  4. To view details of a specific contact, double-click the appropriate contact entry in the right-hand panel. The Person Detail form for the specified contact is displayed in View Only mode.

The primary contact assigned to a customer can be viewed using the Customer Detail form. The form also allows a list of all other contacts assigned to that customer to be viewed using the Other Contacts... button.

To search for a contact using the Customer Detail form:

  1. View the appropriate customer's details.
  2. To display details of the primary contact assigned to this customer, open the Contact tab.

    Note:

    The Contact tab does not display all details recorded for the primary contact. If the required details are not displayed, click the More... button to display the Person Detail form.

  3. To display details of any additional contacts assigned to this customer, click the Other Contacts... button on the Contact tab. The Customer Contacts form is displayed in View Only mode with summary details for the assigned contacts (other than the primary contact).
  4. To view details of a specific contact, double-click the appropriate contact entry in the list, or select the entry and click the Details... button.

Record a New Contact

Details of a new contact must be entered on the Person Detail form, which can be accessed from the:

  • Customer Care menu, by selecting the Customer Care People New Person option.
  • Customer Detail form, by clicking the New... button on the Contact tab.

The Person Search form is displayed, for recording details for the new contact.

To record details for a new contact:

  1. On the Person Search form, enter any relevant information about the contact in the search criteria fields.
  2. Select the Create a new person option and click the New… button. Convergent Billing performs a search of the database and then displays either the Person Detail or Person List form.

    When the New… button on the Person Search form is clicked, Convergent Billing performs a search of the database based on the information specified on the search form.

    • If no records are found that match the criteria, the Person Detail form is displayed in Insert mode.
    • If one or more records that match the information about the search form are found, those records are displayed on the Person List form. Details of each record (using the Details… button) can then be displayed, and if an appropriate match does not exist, a new record can be created by clicking the New… button on the Person List form; the Person Detail form is then displayed.

    Any information entered on the Person Search form is inserted into the appropriate fields on the Person Detail form.

  3. If the Person List form is displayed, view each of the records by selecting each record in turn and clicking the Details… button. If an appropriate match does not exist, click the New… button. The Person Detail form is displayed in Insert mode.
  4. Complete the fields in the upper part of the Person Detail form.
  5. Select the Address & Phone tab, and enter at least one address and one phone number.
  6. If required, complete the fields on the Other and General tabs.

    Note:

    Depending on the person type selected, the General tab may not contain any fields.

  7. Click the Save button on the toolbar. If all mandatory fields have been completed, the details are saved and the form is reopened in View Only mode.

Assign a Primary Customer Contact

A primary contact is assigned as part of creating a customer.

A primary customer contact can be changed if required.

Change Primary Customer Contact

To replace (change) the primary customer contact:

  1. View the appropriate customer's details.
  2. Open the Contact tab. Details of the current primary customer contact are displayed.
  3. Click the Update button on the toolbar. The Customer Detail form is displayed in Update mode.

    Note:

    Depending on available privileges and the value in the Start Date field of the current record, a Confirm window may be displayed.

    Update privileges required for types of update:

    Type of Update

    Privilege Required

    Used To

    Normal

    Update

    Update current and future records.

    Historical Update

    Update an historical record, or the current record and backdate the changes.

    Date range

    Date Range Update

    Modify the date range of an historical record.

     

  4. If details for the replacement contact are recorded in the system, click the Search... button. The Person Search form is displayed.
  5. Search for and display the details of the replacement contact in the Person Detail form.

    Tip:

    If the search retrieves more than one contact, the Person List form is displayed. Select the replacement contact from the list and click the Assign button on the Person List form. A message is displayed to confirm that the nominated contact is to be assigned to the specified customer. Continue at step 6 of this procedure.

  6. If details for the replacement contact are not recorded in the system, click the New... button. The Person Search form is displayed.
  7. Enter any relevant information about the contact in the search criteria fields and click the New… button. The Person Detail form is displayed in Insert mode.
  8. Record the details for the replacement contact and then save them. A message is displayed prompting confirmation that the nominated contact is to be assigned to the specified customer.
  9. Click the Yes button to replace the details of the current primary customer contact with those of the replacement contact.
  10. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Assign a Secondary Customer Contact

To assign a secondary customer contact:

  1. View the appropriate customer's details.
  2. Click the Other Contacts... button on the Contact tab. The Customer Contacts form is displayed in View Only mode, listing the secondary contacts (if any) assigned to the specified customer.
  3. Click the Insert button on the toolbar. The Customer Contacts form is displayed in Update mode with the cursor positioned at the start of a blank row.
  4. If details for the contact to be assigned are recorded in the system, click the Search... button. The Person Search form is displayed. Search for and display the details of the contact to be assigned in the Person Detail form.

    Tip:

    If the search retrieves more than one contact, the Person List form is displayed. Select the replacement contact from the list and click the Assign button on the Person List form. A prompt is displayed to confirm that the nominated contact is to be assigned to the specified customer. Continue at step 6 of this procedure.

  5. If details for the contact are not recorded in the system, click the New... button. The Person Search form is displayed. Alternatively, the Person List form may be displayed if the details entered on the Person Search form are similar to another person in the database. Record the details for the contact and then save the details. A message is displayed prompting confirmation to assign the nominated contact to the specified customer.
  6. Click the Yes button.
  7. Repeat steps 3 to 6 for each contact to be assigned to the customer.
  8. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Change a Secondary Customer Contact

To change (replace) a secondary customer contact:

  1. View the appropriate customer's details.
  2. Click the Other Contacts... button on the Contact tab. The Customer Contacts form is displayed in View Only mode, listing the secondary contacts (if any) assigned to the specified customer.
  3. Click the Update button on the toolbar. The Customer Contacts form is displayed in Update mode.

    Note:

    Depending on available privileges and the value in the Start Date field of the current record, a Confirm window may be displayed before the Customer Contacts form is displayed.

  4. If details for the replacement contact have been recorded in the system, click the Search... button. The Person Search form is displayed. Search for and display the details of the replacement contact in the Person Detail form.

    Tip:

    If the search retrieves more than one contact, the Person List form is displayed. Select the replacement contact from the list and click the Assign button on the Person List form. A message is displayed for confirmation that the nominated contact be assigned to the specified customer. Continue at step 6 of this procedure.

  5. If details for the replacement contact are not recorded in the system, click the New... button. The Person Search form is displayed. Enter any relevant information about the contact in the search criteria fields and click the New… button. The Person Detail form is displayed in Insert mode. Record the details for the replacement contact and then save those details.

    A message is displayed for confirmation that the nominated contact be assigned to the specified customer.

  6. Click the Yes button to replace the contact. Click the No or Cancel button to leave the existing contact.
  7. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Remove a Customer Contact

A primary customer contact cannot be removed, only replaced (changed). Secondary customer contacts can be removed, replaced, or modified whenever required.

To remove (unassign) a secondary customer contact:

  1. View the appropriate customer's details.
  2. Click the Other Contacts... button on the Contact tab. The Customer Contacts form is displayed listing the secondary contacts (if any) assigned to the specified customer.
  3. Select the row containing the contact to be removed.
  4. Click the Delete button on the toolbar. The Customer Contacts form is placed in Update mode and the specified contact is removed.

    Note:

    Depending on privileges and the value in the Start Date field, a Confirm window may be displayed before the Customer Contacts form is displayed.

  5. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Update a Contact

If sufficient update privileges are available, changes can be made to a contact record, and backdated.

To update a contact's details:

  1. Display the appropriate Person Detail form.
  2. Click the History button on the toolbar. The Person History form for the specified contact is displayed in View Only mode.
  3. Examine the date ranges of the records in the list of historical records (if more than one) to determine whether an update is required to an historical, current, or future record.
  4. If an update to an historical or future record is required, double-click the appropriate entry (the entry having the date range spanning the date to which the changes are to be backdated). The Person Detail form is displayed with details for the specified contact record.
  5. Click the Update button on the toolbar. The Person Detail form is displayed in Update mode.

    Note:

    Depending on update privileges and the start date of the record, a Confirm window may be displayed.

  6. Update the person details as required.
  7. Click the Save button on the toolbar. If all mandatory fields have been completed, the details are saved, the message 'Changes saved successfully' is displayed on the status bar, and the form is reopened in View Only mode.

Delete a Contact

Rules for Deleting a Contact

A contact record can be deleted only if:

  • The record is not assigned as the primary contact for a customer or customer node.

    Main or initial contact associated with a customer. If more than one contact is listed for a specific customer, one of those contacts is specified as the primary contact.

    Context: Convergent Billing, Customer Management

    See also Contact and Secondary Contact.

  • A query is not assigned to that contact.
    Any customer enquiry recorded and subsequently processed is referred to as a query. Figure 10 displays the query process.

     

    Figure 10 Query Process

     

    A query can be recorded against a customer (existing or potential) or, more rarely, against a contact. Recording a query against a contact is used to accurately record information reported by customers, and for an organisation to obtain statistical information on the types of inquiry being handled and the frequency of each type.

    A query must first be recorded (usually by the person who deals directly with the customer making the enquiry) and then either resolved immediately or forwarded electronically to a work group to be resolved. The act of forwarding the query to a work group is referred to as progressing the query.

    If the designated work group cannot resolve the query, or can only provide a partial answer, that work group progresses (forwards) the query to another work group. Progression continues until the initial query is resolved and a response is made to the customer.

    The progression of a query from creation to resolution, using one or more work groups, is referred to as the query workflow. Figure 11 displays the query progression.

     

    Figure 11 Query Progression

     

    Query workflow ensures that each query is resolved in the fastest possible time and in the most efficient manner, regardless of the query type.

    While a query remains unresolved, it is regarded as being open. Until it is resolved, it is regarded as being closed.

    When a query is forwarded to a work group, that work group actually receives a copy of the query and uses the copy as their ‘working’ document. The ‘working’ document is referred to as a progression and is used to record any actions taken by the work group and, where possible, the solution to the original query.

    Document used to record the actions taken by a work group to resolve a query. If the query cannot be resolved by the current work group, that work group adds resolution text to the progression before forwarding the query to another work group.

    Each progression therefore contains progress text (detailing the actions taken by a previous work group) and resolution text (detailing the actions performed by the current work group to resolve the query).

    Context: Convergent Billing

    See also Progress, Progress Text, Query Text, and Resolution Text.

    To progress a query:
    1. View the appropriate customer query.
    2. Click the Progress button. A message is displayed, prompting confirmation that the query is to be progressed to the specified work force. By default, the work force displayed is that associated with the query type selected.
    3. If required, select an alternative work force.
    4. Click Yes to progress the query to the nominated work force.

    Need more information on queries?

    If the query cannot be resolved by the nominated work group, that work group adds text to the progression (known as resolution text) stating what actions were taken and what progress was made, and forwards a copy of the progression to the next work group. The resolution text copied from the previous progression becomes the progress text on which the new work group bases its actions. The process of forwarding a copy of a progression to the next work group continues until a work group can resolve the query. At this point, a copy of the text stating how the query can be resolved is recorded against the original query and the query is deemed to be closed. The query workflow mechanism ensures that each progression and the original query are retained and can be viewed.

    Text on which a work group bases its actions. The progress text is created as resolution text by the previous work group before progressing the query to the current work group. For the initial progression, the progress text is a copy of the query text.

    Context: Convergent Billing

    See also Progress, Progression, Query Text, and Resolution Text.

    Text added to a progression by a work group. If this work group has not resolved the query, the resolution text becomes the progress text for the next work group.

    Context: Convergent Billing

    See also Progress Text, Progression, and Query Text.

  • The contact is not in use in the date range specified.

Note:

A contact can be assigned to more than one customer record (as a primary or secondary contact). Each primary contact assignment must be removed before the specified contact record can be deleted.

Person, other than the primary contact, assigned as an additional contact for a customer.

Context: Convergent Billing

See also Customer Contact and Primary Contact.

To delete a contact record:

  1. View the appropriate Person Detail form.
  2. Click the Delete button on the toolbar. A Confirm window is displayed.
  3. Specify the date from which to delete the contact. Depending on security settings, the date consists of a single component, the date, or two components, the date and time.
  4. Click the Yes button to delete the contact record. To cancel the request to delete the specified contact, click the No or Cancel button.

Create a Query

A query can be created for a:

  • Customer
  • Prospective customer (for example, a person requesting information about services), or a contact.

The procedure to create a query is similar for both types of customer, except that for potential customers and contacts the name and address of a contact need to be specified. The details are entered automatically by Convergent Billing if a query is created for an existing customer.

Individual Adjustment Form

The Individual Adjustment form is used to enter, view, and update adjustment details.

Transaction outside of the normal billing cycle transactions (namely, invoices and payments) that affects the outstanding balance of a customer's account. An adjustment is recorded against a specific customer's account and optionally against one or more specified invoices.

Debit and credit notes are commonly referred to as adjustments in Convergent Billing.

Context: Convergent Billing, Singleview

See also Adjustment Batch, Adjustment Type, Payment, Debit Note, and Credit Note.

The upper part of the form contains the Adjustment Number and Adjustment Batch Number (optionally) read-only fields.

The lower part of the form consists of the following tabs, for entering details about the current adjustment:

Details

The Details tab is used to specify the adjustment type, adjustment amount, the date the adjustment was received, and the account to which the adjustment is to be posted.

Text to be displayed on the customer’s invoice or statement (Adjustment Text field), and additional non-printed comments (Description field) can be entered.

Note:

An adjustment amount can only be entered up to a user's predefined adjustment limit, and in the specified currency.

The Details tab is also used to associate a query to the adjustment. Facilities are provided to search for, view, and assign the appropriate query.

If this form is being used to view a recorded adjustment and the adjustment was recorded as part of an adjustment batch, facilities are provided to view the associated adjustment batch.

General

The General tab contains general information about the adjustment. The details in this section are dependent on the adjustment type associated with the adjustment. If present, do not change the displayed details.

The Debit Account Details group may be completed by Convergent Billing based on the adjustment type selected and the type of account associated with the account specified. If the adjustment type explicitly specifies a debit account type, the debit account number is set automatically and cannot be updated. If the adjustment type does not specify a debit account type, a debit account number cannot be entered.

Note:

The details displayed in the Debit Account Details group depends on the site configuration.

Allocations

The Allocations tab is used to:

  • List outstanding invoices, adjustments, or payments associated with the account set in the Credit Account Number field on the Detail tab.
  • Allocate the adjustment (defined on the Details tab) to one or more of the displayed invoices, adjustments, and payments.

Note:

The Allocations tab is displayed when all the following conditions are met:

  • The user's form role includes the View Allocations access level for the Individual Adjustment form.
  • The Credit Account Number field on the Details tab specifies a prime account.
  • Either the Currency or Amount field on the Details tab contains a value.

A Receivable Type column is displayed only if an invoice, adjustment, or payment on the tab is divided into receivable types or a credit amount is assigned to receivable types.

The tab displays items according to these rules:

  • An invoice, adjustment, or payment with a zero total amount outstanding is displayed if the invoice has receivable types with non-zero outstanding amounts.
  • If an invoice, adjustment, or payment is divided into multiple receivable types, each division is displayed on a separate row in the grid with the Receivable Type column set.
  • If an invoice, adjustment, or payment has a receivable type, the Total Amount and Amount Due columns display the values for the receivable type. Otherwise, the values are for are the amounts for invoice, adjustment or payment.
  • If a credit amount is divided into multiple receivable types, each receivable type is shown on a separate row with empty invoice-related columns. Any credit amount not assigned to a receivable type is not displayed in a row on the tab.

Inserting or Updating a Payment

When inserting or updating an adjustment, the tab displays:

  • Invoices
    • Outstanding invoices, including invoices with an issue date after the value entered in the payment's Date Received field.
    • Invoices paid as part of this individual adjustment.
  • Adjustments
    • Outstanding adjustments for the credit account.
    • Adjustments paid as part of this individual adjustment.
  • Payments
    • Outstanding payments for the credit account
    • Payments paid as part of this individual adjustment.

Note:

All outstanding invoices, adjustments, and payments must be finalised before any adjustment amount is left unallocated as credit.

The adjustment can be allocated in the following ways:

  • To a single invoice, adjustment, or payment
  • Split between a number of invoices, adjustments, or payments
  • Credited to a specified account.

Note:

New individual adjustment amounts can be entered against each invoice, adjustment, or payment but the value must be between zero and the value of the Amount Due field inclusive.

The This Adjustment column on the Allocations tab can be edited if the Update Invoice Allocations access level is selected for the Individual Adjustment form in a user's form role.

Debit Account Allocations

The Debit Account Allocations tab lists outstanding invoices, adjustments, or payments associated with the debit account.

The tab is also used to allocate an adjustment (defined on the Details tab) to one or more of the listed invoices, adjustments, or payments.

An adjustment can be allocated as follows:

  • To a single invoice, adjustment, or payment
  • Split between a number of invoices, adjustments, or payments
  • Credited to a specified account.

When displaying an individual adjustment, only those invoices, allocated adjustments, and payments that have been paid as part of this adjustment, and credit amounts assigned to receivable types, are displayed.

Note:

The tab is displayed only if the View Invoice Allocations access level is selected for the Individual Adjustment form in a user's form role and the Debit Account Number on the Details tab is a prime account.

The This Adjustment column on the tab can be edited if the Update Invoice Allocations access level is selected for the Individual Adjustment form in a user's form role.

For information about setting form role access levels, refer to Update a Form Role.

Currency Conversion

The Currency Conversion tab contains information for making a currency conversion.

The Conversion Date field defaults to the Adjustment Date field on the Details tab and is updated when the Adjustment Date field is modified. If the Conversion Date field is updated by the user, it is no longer updated when the Adjustment Date field is modified. Conversions are possible for the currencies set in the accounts specified by the Credit Account Number and Debit Account Number fields.

The Currency Conversion tab is visible only if the currency of the adjustment differs to that of the account in the Credit Account Number field.

Image

The Image tab contains status information for the adjustment image, and the option to regenerate or view the adjustment image.

By default, all fields on the tab are read-only.

The View Image button is only enabled if the Image Generated checkbox is selected.

The Generate Image button is only enabled if the Image Format field contains a value.

Note:

The Image tab is displayed only if configured for the selected adjustment type.

Note:

The Adjustment Number field is assigned automatically by Convergent Billing when the adjustment is saved.

Associated Actions

Viewing an Adjustment

To view an adjustment:

  1. Select the Accounts Adjustments Manage Individual Adjustments option. The Adjustment Detail Search form is displayed.
  2. Enter or select the appropriate search criteria.

    Tip:

    If the adjustment date is unknown, remove the date from the Adjustment Date field.

  3. If appropriate, select the Always Display List? option.

    If a single adjustment record is returned from a search, the details of that record are displayed on the Individual Adjustment Detail form. If more than one adjustment record is returned, the records are listed on the Adjustment Detail List form. The required record can then be selected and its details displayed on the Individual Adjustment Detail form.

    If selected, the Always Display List? option overrides the automatic display of the Individual Adjustment Detail form when only a single record is retrieved; in such cases the Adjustment Detail List form is displayed.

  4. Click the Search… button.

If the search returns:

  • A single record, the Individual Adjustment form is displayed (if the Always Display List? option was not selected).
  • Multiple records, or the Always Display List? option was selected, the Adjustment List form is displayed. To access the Individual Adjustment form from the Adjustment List form, double-click the appropriate adjustment in the list of retrieved adjustments, or select the appropriate adjustment and click the Details… button. The Individual Adjustment form is displayed in View Only mode.

Recording an Adjustment

Adjustments can be entered in either of the following ways:

  • Individually
  • In batches of the same adjustment type.

Before entering an adjustment, ensure that accurate information about the customer is available, amount of the adjustment, and the adjustment type. The details need to be recorded or verified to successfully enter an adjustment.

Tip:

When entering an adjustment, it is useful to have the Customer Detail form open for the customer getting the adjustment. The customer's account number can then be copied from the Customer Detail form and pasted into the Account Number field on the relevant form.

Reversing an Adjustment

Tip:

An adjustment can only be reversed once it is posted to a customer's account. Therefore, an adjustment made as part of an adjustment batch can only be reversed if that batch is applied. Adjustments included in an adjustment batch that is not yet applied can simply be removed from the batch.

To reverse an adjustment:

  1. Enter an adjustment of equal (but opposite) value to the adjustment to be reversed.
  2. In the Description field, cross-reference the adjustment number of the reversed adjustment.

Deleting an Adjustment

To delete an adjustment not posted to a customer's account:

  1. View the appropriate adjustment.
  2. Click the Delete button on the toolbar.
  3. When prompted, click the Yes button to confirm that the adjustment is to be deleted.

An adjustment posted to a customer's account can also be deleted using the same procedure if:

  • The adjustment is not included on (appears on) an invoice or statement.
  • The adjustment has not been posted to the Sales Journal.
  • Security privileges are available.

If deletion is not possible, the adjustment should be reversed and the adjustment entered against the correct account.

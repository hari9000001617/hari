Products

When a customer’s details have been recorded in Convergent Billing, the customer can be assigned one or more products. Convergent Billing supports two categories of product:

  • Base products, which are combinations of services, tariffs (charges), subtotals (aggregated charges or discounts), facility groups (sets of service options), and derived attributes (types of value lists) required to deliver a product package that is tailored to the specific requirements of a customer. Base products may be associated with a product offer.
  • Companion products, which add tariffs (charges) and list objects to allow an alternative tariff regime to be applied to a base product.

Each base product has associated with it at least one, and possibly many, services (and any associated equipment).

Provides a specific component of the overall product for which the customer is charged. Each service has an associated service type, and unique service number against which usage is charged. One or more facilities (service options) or items of equipment, or both, can be associated with each service.

Context: Convergent Billing

See also Equipment, Facility, and Service Type.

Note:

At least one service associated with a product must be created, to activate the product instance.

When a base product with an associated product offer is assigned to a customer, companion products and child product offers are made available for selection. A certain number of each may be required to be added to the product offer.

If the base product is not associated with a product offer, one or more facility groups (sets of service options) and one or more companion products can be specified for the product.

Product used to add tariffs, subtotals, and derived attributes to a base product. Allows an alternative costing regime to be applied to an existing product. A companion product cannot exist in isolation; it must be associated with an existing base product.

Context: Convergent Billing, Customer Management

See also Base Product, Base Product Compatibility, Companion Product Compatibility and Feature.

Collection of one or more service options (known as facilities) defined in a product and associated with a specified service. A facility group must contain at least one facility, and can also contain details of compatible facility groups.

Context: Convergent Billing, Customer Management, Configuration Studio

See also Facility and Facility Group Compatibility.

Each product definition has an associated product icon by which that product is identified in a product group.

Icon associated with a specific base product. The same icon can be used for more than one product. Product icons are displayed in product groups on the Customer Hierarchy form.

Context: Convergent Billing

See also Base Product and Product Group.

During the process of assigning a product to a customer, a copy of the product definition is made and tailored to meet a specific customer’s requirement. The copy or product instance of the product definition is unique within the Convergent Billing database.

Specific combination of services and the associated tariff structure offered to a customer based on the framework provided by the corresponding product definition. The original product definition is preserved, with the product instance being tailored to a specific customer's requirements. Any changes made to the product definition affect only product instances created after the changes are made, not existing product instances.

Referred to as a Purchased Product in Customer Management.

Context: Convergent Billing, Customer Management

See also Product Definition and Product Model.

More than one active instance of the same companion product can be assigned to a single base product using one or more product offers or directly, if specified in the definition of the companion product. Having the same companion product assigned to a single base product is only useful for activation, cancellation, entitlements, and recurring charges generated by the RGP, because multiple companion products of the same name do not influence rating or billing behaviour.

A completed product instance has a status of Active. A product instance is completed when the following conditions are met:

  • It has at least one Active service.
  • All mandatory services and equipment have been created and allocated.
  • Optional services and equipment that have been added, are created and allocated.

A product instance is automatically completed and its status changed to Active when:

  • The maximum number of services and equipment have been created.
  • Optional components have not been specified.

A product instance that is not yet complete (that is, a product having a status of Product Creation) can be deleted.

When a product instance is created for a customer, that instance can be modified whenever required. For example, initially the customer may only want to use one of the available facility groups, then at a later date request the use of additional facility groups.

A product instance can change to be incomplete while in a status of Active if:

  • New optional services or equipment are added manually, but not created or allocated.
  • A product offer changes by:
    • Adding new mandatory services or equipment.
    • Reducing the maximum permitted number of services for a specific service type, leaving too many services allocated.

After all new services and equipment have been assigned, the product instance is automatically re-completed.

Cancelling a product instance changes the status of the product instance and each of its constituent services, facility groups, and companion products to Cancelled. At some later time, the product instance and its associated services can be deleted if all outstanding items for the product and its services have been completed.

Product instances that are not associated with product offers can also be upgraded; this requires a different base product to be assigned to the customer as a replacement for their product.

Upgrading refers to replacing the product on which the product instance is based. Replacing the base product can therefore result in an upgrade or downgrade of the base product.

A product instance must be replaced if:

  • The base product does not meet the customer’s new requirements.
  • Companion products (if any) for the base product do not allow the customer’s new requirements to be met.

A new service number usually needs to be assigned when a base product assigned to a customer needs to be replaced. For example, if a customer is assigned a GSM phone with voice-only capability and that customer wants to upgrade to a GSM phone with voice and data capability, a new product must be assigned to replace the product, and also a new service number.

Note:

A base product can only be replaced with a compatible base product.

If required, product instances can also be moved between customer nodes in the same hierarchy or between nodes in different hierarchies.

A base product instance (and its associated services and companion product instances) can be moved from one customer node to another in a customer hierarchy (some restrictions apply). A move is achieved by dragging the required product instance from its current customer node (referred to as the source node) and dropping it onto another node (referred to as the target node). The source node and target node can be in the same customer hierarchy or different hierarchies.

The following restrictions apply when moving a product instance:
  • A product cannot be upgraded or moved if the product instance was created as part of a product offer.
  • A product cannot be moved if its owning customer or target customer is blacklisted at any time during the move period and the user does not have access to view blacklisted customers on the Customer Form.
  • A product cannot be upgraded or moved if the product instance is cancelled, moved, or upgraded at the effective start date or any later date.
  • A product must be assigned to a customer node. A product dragged over a root node or blank space of the hierarchy panel cannot be dropped.
  • Some information regarding the lists and charge categories may need to be entered (when prompted) before the product instance move can be completed.

Caution:

If a product instance is moved, any changes made to it before or after the move start date are not carried over in the move. For example, if the product instance is moved at an effective date before an equipment allocation change, following the move the variation of equipment allocation is not reflected. A warning is displayed so the user can cancel the move.

When dropped onto the target node, customer node lists, customer lists, customer node charge categories, customer charge categories, and start date are generated for the target node as though a new product instance was being created for that node. Some information regarding charge categories may therefore need to be entered (when prompted) before the product instance move can be completed successfully. If a product instance is moved on the same day it is created, any activation charges are carried over to the new customer node.

Because of the move, the service accounts are mapped from the source customer node to equivalent accounts on the target customer node. Any non-invoiced usage charges posted on or after the date the product instance is moved, are moved across to the new accounts. Recurring charges, such as rentals, are posted to the new accounts the next time the Rental Generation Process (RGP) is run as part of the normal billing cycle.

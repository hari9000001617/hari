This guide is designed to assist Convergent Billing administrators and other personnel responsible for ensuring users working in customer care have the necessary information and facilities to perform their tasks.

The guide also provides Convergent Billing product administrators with the information they need to design and define a product.

Who Should Read this Guide?

This guide is intended for:

  • Customer care administrators who:
    • Customise Convergent Billing
    • Provide assistance with configuration of Convergent Billing for customer care operations
    • Provide and manage data entry facilities for customer care tasks
    • Provide users with access to various areas of customer care and associated data records.
  • Product and tariff designers, or developers who design, define, and implement products using Convergent Billing.

Is Prerequisite Knowledge Assumed?

This guide assumes:

  • Experience in using Convergent Billing
  • Experience in using Microsoft Windows
  • Thorough knowledge of work procedures and business rules for customer care, and of products offered to customers, or ready access to that information
  • Knowledge of SQL (for product administrators requiring access to database tables)
  • Some knowledge of programming or writing expressions, because Convergent Billing uses expressions to define tariffs and subtotals
  • Knowledge of tariffs and tariff regimes for understanding Convergent Billing's flexible tariff definition facilities

Note:

The reader should be familiar with the Configuration Fundamentals Guide for Singleview Convergent Billing.

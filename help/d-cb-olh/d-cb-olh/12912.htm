Financial Capabilities

Convergent Billing provides the following accounting functions:

  • Customer account management, including maintaining customer account balances and history.
  • Accounts Receivable (A/R) sub-ledger.
  • Sales and Cash Receipts Journal (in Singleview, referred to as a Sales Journal), including revenue recognition (revenue, accrued revenue, and deferred revenue).

Convergent Billing processes affecting Convergent Billing accounts and the Sales Journal are:

  • Rating
  • Billing
  • Payment processing
  • Adjustment processing.

When a transaction occurs during any of these processes, Convergent Billing records the effects of that transaction on both the internal Convergent Billing customer account balance and history, and the general ledger accounts (for later processing by the Sales Journal posting process).

When the Sales Journal posting process is run, transactions from rating, billing, payments, and adjustments are aggregated. Aggregation is based on transaction type (for example, invoice or payment) and debit and credit GL codes, and an entry made in the Sales Journal. Convergent Billing determines the GL code for:

  • Normalised event file and invoice transactions, during the Sales Journal posting process, using a process known as GL guidance.
  • Payment and adjustment transactions, when the payment or adjustment is applied.

At the end of an accounting period, the Sales Journal entries are aggregated and uploaded to the external general ledger system.

For more information about GL guidance, refer to the Finance Guide for Singleview.

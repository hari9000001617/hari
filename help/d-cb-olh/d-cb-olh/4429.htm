How Do I - Security Tasks - Form Roles

Select the option most appropriate to the task.

Define a Form Role

To define a form role:

  1. Select the Maintenance Security Form Roles option. The Form Role Search form is displayed.
  2. Click the New… button. The Form Role Definition form is displayed in Insert mode.
  3. Complete the Name and Description fields.
  4. Complete a line in the grid for each menu item or form to which the form role is to provide access.
  5. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

View a Form Role

To view a form role:

  1. Select the Maintenance Security Form Roles option. The Form Role Search form is displayed.
  2. Enter or select the appropriate search criteria and search method.
  3. Click the Search button. A list of the defined roles matching the specified criteria is displayed.
  4. Select the appropriate role and click the Detail… button, or double-click the appropriate role. The Form Role form is displayed in View Only mode.

Need more information on security?

View Access Privileges on a Form Role

To view the access privileges of a form:

  1. View the desired form role.
  2. Select the desired form and click on the Access Level Settings button. The access levels for that form are displayed.

Note:

The descriptions below highlight the difference between a detail record and a record:

  • A record is an entity being viewed in a form. For example, if the Service Detail form is being used, the record is of a service.
  • A detail record is data that relates to the original record, but which belongs to another entity. Using the same example, the charge categories that can be viewed on the Service Detail form are detail records.

Update a Form Role

To update a form role:

  1. View the appropriate form role.
  2. Click the Update button on the toolbar. The Form Role form is displayed in Update mode.
  3. Update the form role details as required:
    • Click on the Insert button to insert a new line in the grid for a menu item or form to which the form role is to provide access.
    • Highlight a form and click on the Access Level Settings button to display the access levels for that form. Select the appropriate access levels for the form.
  4. Click the Save button on the toolbar.

Need more information on security?

Delete a Form Role

To delete a form role:

  1. View the appropriate form role.
  2. Click the Delete button on the toolbar.
  3. When prompted, click the Yes button to confirm that the form role is to be deleted.

Enable Date-time Granularity

To enable date-time granularity, use the Update a Form Role option to change the access level for the forms for the relevant entity by selecting the Allow date range time granularity of less than one day access level check box.

For a list of form codes, associated form descriptions and form fields affected by the Allow date range time granularity of less than one day access level, refer to the System Configuration Guide for Singleview Convergent Billing.

Note:

  • Date-time granularity is configured using form roles, so the same form can have different granularity for different users. To avoid user confusion, it is recommended that a form's date-time granularity be configured the same in all form roles.
  • Because the TRE is not subject to form role security, TRE functions can specify time components for dates, regardless of the form role configuration. When an entity's date fields are updated by TRE functions, if the dates :
    • Require time components, enable date-time granularity for users.
    • Do not require time components, disable date-time granularity for users, and ensure that TRE functions are called without time components in parameters.

Disable Date-time Granularity

To disable date-time granularity for a form, update the form role by clearing the Allow date range time granularity of less than one day access level for each form, as required.

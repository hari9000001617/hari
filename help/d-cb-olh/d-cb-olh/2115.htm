Invoices

An invoice contains the charges owing for services the customer has used.

Convergent Billing is used to view:

  • Details of each invoice generated for a specific customer. The details include invoice number, account number, invoiced amount, issue date, any early payment discounts, and details of subtotals and taxes (if any) displayed on the invoice.
  • An electronic copy (image) of the printed invoice sent to the customer to identify the charge or item being enquired about by a customer.
  • An image of the invoice immediately after it was generated.

Invoices are only generated (as part of a bill run) if the report level of the customer node is set to Invoice, and are only raised against the customer's primary account. The frequency at which a customer is sent an invoice is determined by the invoice cycle requested by that customer. Interim invoices (generated using the Interim Bill Run schedule type) only include rental events that have been designated as interim charges.

Generated at times other than the regular invoice cycle and includes charges defined as 'interim'. Interim invoices are typically used for activation tariffs (when the service provider wants to charge the installation or activation fees as soon as the service is installed or requested).

Context: Convergent Billing

See also Invoice, Invoice Cycle, and Tariff.

Invoices can be suppressed if they meet configurable criteria. Invoice images are not produced for suppressed invoices.

The appearance of an invoice, the level of detail it contains, and the frequency at which it is generated are specified for a customer when that customer’s details are recorded in Convergent Billing.

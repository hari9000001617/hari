Contract Type

Special terms and conditions can be applied to a set of products and services for any root customer by using contracts. The contract functionality enhances the product administrator's ability to design flexible products by enabling them to include features such as the following:

Legal agreement between the organisation and the customer to apply special terms and conditions to products and services for a specific duration.

Context: Convergent Billing

Provides a specific component of the overall product for which the customer is charged. Each service has an associated service type, and unique service number against which usage is charged. One or more facilities (service options) or items of equipment, or both, can be associated with each service.

Context: Convergent Billing

See also Equipment, Facility, and Service Type.

Refer to Base Product and Companion Product.

  • Products at a discount price for a specified period of time
  • Discounts for selected ranges of numbers (for example, a company's calls between offices)
  • Fixed prices for specific products
  • Specific contracts to meet requirements for an individual customer.

A contract is implemented by defining a base product (to include the basic product requirements) and a companion product and associating them with a contract type. The companion product is used to modify the costing attributes of the base product for the term of the contract. The companion product can be used to add tariff definition/charge category pairs and subtotal definitions used in conjunction with those in the base product. Discount tariffs can also be added to change the charges calculated as a result of applying the base product tariffs.

Identifies the default account type to which a calculated tariff charge or benefit is posted (allocated). For services, the charge category also identifies a specific account number. The association between a tariff and a charge category is specified when the tariff is defined and is referred to as the tariff/charge category pair.

By default, the charge category associated with a tariff is used each time that tariff is used. However, the charge category can be overridden when defining a customer type, service type, customer node, or service.

Context: Convergent Billing, Configuration Studio

See also Account Type and Tariff.

Table of conditions and associated charges. The table is used to determine the charge values to be substituted into the charge expression to calculate the value of the specified tariff. The charge values used for the substitution are those associated with the conditions in the tariff table that match the conditional expressions used as keys into the tariff table.

The structure of the tariff table is created during tariff configuration and the appropriate conditions and charges entered into the table during tariff definition.

Context: Convergent Billing

See also Tariff, Tariff Configuration, and Tariff Definition.

Product used to add tariffs, subtotals, and derived attributes to a base product. Allows an alternative costing regime to be applied to an existing product. A companion product cannot exist in isolation; it must be associated with an existing base product.

Context: Convergent Billing, Customer Management

See also Base Product, Base Product Compatibility, Companion Product Compatibility and Feature.

Combination of service types, equipment types, tariffs, subtotals, facility groups, and derived attributes required to deliver a competitive product offering. A product is the only entity sold to a customer.

Context: Convergent Billing, Configuration Studio

See also Base Product Compatibility, Companion Product, and Companion Product Compatibility.

For example, a twelve month contract is defined to offer a discount, to large corporate customers, on mobile calls if a certain usage for their standard business touch phones is reached. A companion product is defined with a discount tariff that overrides the usage tariffs, and any subtotals, specified in the base product. Tariff eligibility criteria is applied to determine whether the set amount of usage has been reached and whether or not the discount is applied.

To define a product that includes a contract, a base product and companion product are defined and then applied to a specific contract type. When a user applies a contract to a customer record, the selected contract type determines the products and companion products used for the contract. When the contract is assigned to one or more service instances on the base product instance, the companion product is created automatically.

The contract type is also used to define the following defaults for contracts of that type:

  • Duration of the contract.
  • Grace period (the amount of time after the expected expiry date that the contract can remain active before being cancelled or re-negotiated).
  • Account manager responsible for the contract.
  • Re-negotiation period (the period of time before the contract expires that the account manager is notified re-negotiation is required).
  • Expiry warning period (the period of time before the expiry of the contract when the account manager is warned that the contract is about to expire).
  • Sales channel (the method by which the contract was obtained).

The contract type is also used to define the defaults for contracts of that type and to apply entity validation to the Contract Detail form.

Invoices for Payment Form

The Invoices for Payment form is used to:

  • List outstanding invoices associated with the specified account.
  • Allocate a payment to one or more of those invoices.
  • Allocate the payment to the account as a credit.

When inserting or updating a payment, all outstanding invoices for this account and any invoices that have been paid as part of a payment are displayed. Amounts up to the amount owing can be allocated to a:

  • Single invoice, where the due amount of the invoice must be less than or equal to the allocated amount.
  • Combination of invoices, where the sum of the total allocation must be less than, or equal to, the combined payment amount.

Closing this form returns control to the Payment Batch form and details about the allocation are inserted in the Invoice column of the table row:

  • If the payment was allocated to a single invoice, the invoice number is inserted.
  • If the payment was allocated to more than one invoice, the word ‘Split’ is inserted.
  • If there were no outstanding invoices or the payment was not allocated to an outstanding invoice, the word ‘Credit’ is inserted.

Associated Action

Creating a Payment Batch

To create a payment batch:

  1. Collate payments into batches (groups) of the same payment type.
  2. Select the Accounts Payments New Batch Payment option. The Payment Batch form is displayed in Insert mode. Alternatively, the form can be displayed by selecting the Create a new batch option on the Payment Batch Search form and clicking the New... button.
  3. Enter a description of this batch in the Description field. The description entry is used to identify the batch at a later date.
  4. Select the appropriate payment type and batch type.
  5. Enter the account or invoice number associated with the payment and press the Tab key. A receipt number is assigned to the payment and displayed in the first field of the current row. The account or invoice number is verified against the account details and, if valid, corresponding account and invoice details are inserted into other fields. If invalid, a warning message is displayed on the Status bar. To rectify this, press the Delete button and enter a valid account or invoice number.

    Tip:

    Ensure that the customer name is correct. If not, a valid, but incorrect account or invoice number has probably been entered. To rectify this, press the Delete button to remove the row, click the Insert button to insert a new row, and re-enter the account or invoice number.

    Note:

    If the payment is being made against a specific account (by entering the account number in the Credit Account Number field), the value that is automatically displayed in the Amount field does not include any unapplied payments or adjustments (for example, future-dated payments). If the payment is being made against a specific invoice (by entering the invoice number in the Invoice field), the value that is automatically displayed in the Amount field includes unapplied payments or adjustments.

  6. If there is a single invoice outstanding against the account, the invoice number and the total amount appears in the Invoice and amount columns respectively. Enter the amount paid in the amount column and press the Tab key.
  7. If there are no outstanding invoices, the word 'Credit' is displayed in the Invoice column. Enter the amount paid in the amount column and press the Tab key.
  8. Click the Invoices… button. The Invoices for payment form is displayed in Insert mode.
  9. If there are a number of invoices against the account, the word 'Split' and the total amount outstanding appear in the Invoice and Amount columns respectively. The Individual Payment form is used to break up the payment between the outstanding invoices associated with the account.
  10. Allocate an amount to one or more of the listed invoices as required.
  11. Press the Tab key or close the Invoices for payment form.
  12. If necessary, change the date on which the payment was received. This may need to be done if payments are entered retrospectively.
  13. Select a value in the Payment Location column.
  14. Complete any other fields defined for this payment.

    Note:

    The fields displayed depend on the payment type selected for the batch. As each payment is entered, the total value of the payments in the batch is displayed on the lower right-hand corner of the form.

  15. Press the Down Arrow key to insert the next payment. A new row is inserted below the current row with a new receipt number.
  16. Repeat steps 5 to 12 until all payments have been entered, or until the batch needs to be saved.
  17. Click the Save button on the toolbar to save the details of the payment. A message confirming that the payment was inserted into the batch is displayed on the Status bar. The Payment Batch form is displayed in View Only mode.
  18. To post the payments in the payment batch to their respective accounts, apply the batch.
  19. To close the payment batch without posting the payments, click the Close button on the toolbar.

Accounts

Each customer must have one primary account, the invoice account, which is the account to which invoiced amounts, payments, and adjustments are posted. The primary account is automatically created when a customer is created.

Additional accounts, referred to as secondary accounts, can also be assigned to customers, depending on their requirements. Secondary accounts are set up by clicking the Other Accounts... button on the Customer Detail form. Examples of secondary accounts include:

  • Deposit accounts, for recording customer deposits.
  • Loyalty accounts, for recording loyalty or frequent user points.

Each account has an account type that defines the different types of accounts to which charges are applied. The account type defines whether the account is an asset or a liability, its currency, and aging criteria. The account type of the primary account is determined by the Prime Account Type field on the Customer Type Definition form.

Account numbers are assigned automatically by Convergent Billing if an account number is not specified when a customer’s details are first recorded. The format of the automatically assigned account number (for primary accounts) is predefined in the selected customer type. The format of the automatically assigned account number (for secondary accounts) is predefined in the selected account type.

Customer accounts can be viewed using the Customer Detail form.
